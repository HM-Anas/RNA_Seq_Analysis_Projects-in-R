Time-Course Transcriptomic Analysis Using Likelihood Ratio Test

Author

Anas Jamshed

Published

June 24, 2026

1 Abstract

This study investigates temporal gene expression dynamics under Control, Low Intensity, and High Intensity stimulation conditions using RNA-Seq data. Differential temporal expression patterns were identified using the DESeq2 Likelihood Ratio Test (LRT). Significant genes were clustered based on expression trajectories, enabling characterization of condition-specific transcriptional programs across time.

2 Introduction

Time-series transcriptomic experiments provide insight into dynamic molecular responses that cannot be captured by static differential expression analyses.

Objectives:

  • Identify genes exhibiting significant temporal expression changes.
  • Compare transcriptional responses across Control, Low, and High intensity conditions.
  • Cluster significant genes according to temporal expression patterns.
  • Visualize biological trends through clustering and trajectory analysis.

10 Biological Interpretation

The clustering analysis identified distinct temporal expression trajectories across all experimental conditions.

Key observations:

  • Early-response gene clusters showed rapid activation after stimulation.
  • Sustained-response clusters maintained elevated expression levels.
  • Late-response clusters demonstrated delayed transcriptional responses.
  • High-intensity stimulation produced stronger expression changes than low-intensity stimulation.

These findings suggest dynamic regulation of biological pathways involved in cellular adaptation and response mechanisms.

11 Discussion

The DESeq2 LRT framework successfully identified genes exhibiting significant temporal behavior. Clustering analysis revealed distinct expression trajectories that may correspond to different biological processes activated throughout the experimental timeline.

Comparison between Control, Low, and High conditions indicated intensity-dependent transcriptional responses, highlighting the impact of stimulation strength on gene regulation.

12 Conclusion

This study successfully applied time-course RNA-Seq analysis using DESeq2 LRT.

Major outcomes:

  1. Identification of temporally regulated genes.
  2. Detection of distinct expression trajectory clusters.
  3. Characterization of intensity-specific transcriptional responses.
  4. Visualization of dynamic gene expression patterns.

These results provide insight into molecular mechanisms governing temporal biological responses.
